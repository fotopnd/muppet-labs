# Template: Design Proposal (RFC)

> **Audience:** Technical (engineers, architects, tech leads)
> **Goal:** Persuade
> **Length:** 500–1500 words
>
> **Author instructions:** This document must drive a decision. The reader should know the proposed answer by the end of the Summary section. Every subsequent section exists to justify that answer or surface risks. Do not bury the recommendation.
>
> **Remove all template instructions (lines starting with `>`) from the final document.**

## Summary

> 1 paragraph. State the problem and the proposed decision. A reader who reads only this section should understand what is being proposed and why. Write it last — it is easier to summarise after the full document exists.

## Context and Problem Statement

> Why does this decision need to be made now? What is the current state, and what is wrong with it or what opportunity exists? Be concrete — reference specific friction, cost, or constraint. Do not frame the problem in terms of the solution.

## Proposed Approach

> What is being proposed? Describe the approach in enough detail that a reviewer can evaluate it. Include: what changes, what stays the same, what depends on this decision.
>
> If implementation steps are needed to clarify the approach, include them — but keep this section focused on the what and why, not the how in granular detail.
