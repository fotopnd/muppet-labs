# Template: Executive Summary

> **Audience:** Executive / Stakeholder (directors, VPs, hiring managers, non-technical decision-makers)
> **Goal:** Inform / Persuade
> **Length:** 300–600 words
>
> **Author instructions:** Lead with the conclusion. Every sentence must earn its place — if you can remove it without losing meaning, remove it. Translate all technical statements into outcomes. Anticipate "so what?" after every paragraph. The Appendix section exists precisely so technical detail does not need to live inline.
>
> **Remove all template instructions (lines starting with `>`) from the final document.**

---

# [Project Name]

## The Problem

> 1 paragraph. What situation, gap, or risk motivated this work? Frame it in terms the reader cares about: business impact, risk, mission alignment, competitive context — not technical limitations. A reader who skips every other section will read this one first.

## What We Built

> 1–3 sentences. No jargon. What exists now that did not before? Write the version a non-technical reader can repeat accurately in a meeting. Technical precision lives in the Appendix.

## Why It Matters

> 1 paragraph. What does this enable, prevent, or demonstrate? Connect directly to something the reader cares about: a strategic goal, a compliance requirement, a capability gap, a demonstration of competence. Do not assume the connection is obvious — state it.

## What We Demonstrated

> Concrete outcomes. Numbers when possible. Results a reader can cite. Avoid: "the system performed well." Include: "the evaluation harness ran 817 test cases in under 3 minutes and detected all known refusal failures in the test set."
>
> If the project is a portfolio piece, this section shows what was demonstrated rather than what was shipped. Be clear about the context.

## What Extension Would Require

> Optional — include if the reader may be asked about scaling, productionising, or extending the work. Brief: 2–4 bullet points. Resources, timeline, dependencies.

## Appendix: Technical Details

> Optional — include a pointer or brief section for readers who want depth. Do not embed technical detail in the main body.
